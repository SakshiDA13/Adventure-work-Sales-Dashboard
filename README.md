# Adventure-work-Sales-Dashboard
Adventure Work Analysis Sales Dashboard
Project Purpose & Goal-
To provide a centralized, interactive overview of global sales, profitability, and product performance across multiple regions and timeframes.
To empower executives and sales managers to track high-level Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), identify top-performing products and customers, spot seasonal sales trends, and make data-driven decisions to optimize profit margins and regional distribution.
Tech Stack- 
​Power BI Desktop: End-to-end report development and interactive visualization.
​Power Query: ETL processes, including data cleaning, type conversion, and attribute filtering.
​DAX (Data Analysis Expressions): Custom measures for Total Sales, Total Orders, and Profit Margin.
​Data Modeling: Star schema implementation for efficient cross-filtering between products, customers, and territory tables.
Features-
Dashboard 1: High-Level Sales Overview
KPI Summary Cards: Highlights the health of the business at a glance with four primary metrics: Total Sales ($29.36M), Total Profit ($12.08M), Profit Margin (41.15%), and Total Quantity Sold (60K).

Monthwise Total Sales (Line Chart): Displays a historical sales trend over months. Interestingly, it features an inverted chronological layout, tracking a decline moving from December/November peaks down through the rest of the year.

Weekly Profit & Sales (Waterfall Chart): Breaks down the cumulative profit contribution by day of the week, clearly illustrating how profits build up from Tuesday through Sunday to reach the final total.

Profit vs. Production Cost by Financial Quarter (100% Stacked Bar Chart): Compares the balance between production costs (hovering around 58.7% – 59%) and profits (hovering around 41%) across Q1 through Q4. It indicates remarkable stability in cost structures across quarters.

Total Sales & Profit by Country (Map Visual): Geographically plots performance, highlighting major market hubs across North America, Europe, and Australia.

Dashboard 2: Product & Customer deep drive
Top Performance Banners: Explicitly names the primary drivers of success: Mountain-200 Black, 46 as the Top Product by Profit, and Jordan Turner as the Top Customer by Profit.

Top 5 Sales by Product (Pie Chart): Breaks down the share of the top 5 highest-grossing products, showing a highly competitive split where each accounts for roughly 19% to 20.6% of the top bracket's sales.

Decomposition Tree (Total Profit Breakdown): Traces how the $12.08M profit splits into categories. Bikes heavily dominate the business ($11.51M), with Road Bikes ($5.54M) and Mountain Bikes ($4.51M) acting as the main subcategory engines.

Top 5 Customers by Sales (Horizontal Bar Chart): Showcases individual high-value buyers, led by Jordan Turner ($16.00K).

Total Sales by Model Name (Horizontal Bar Chart):Ranks the overall sales volume of models, confirming that the Mountain-200 ($7.9M) and Road-150 ($5.5M) are the absolute heavy hitters of the inventory.

Business Impact & Insights:
The "Bike-Heavy" Risk: * Insight: Over 95% of total profits come exclusively from Bikes ($11.51M), with Accessories and Clothing barely contributing.
Impact: The business is highly vulnerable to cycling market fluctuations. There is a massive, untapped opportunity to cross-sell and bundle accessories to diversify revenue.

Exceptional Margin Stability: * Insight: The company maintains a healthy 41.15% profit margin that remains perfectly flat and predictable (~59% cost / 41% profit) across all four quarters.
Impact: This proves excellent supply chain stability and strict cost controls, making financial forecasting highly reliable.

Healthy Customer Distribution: * Insight: The top 5 customers share an almost equal purchasing volume (ranging tightly between $13K and $16K).
Impact: Low revenue risk. The business is not dangerously reliant on a single "whale" client, creating a stable, well-distributed customer base ripe for a VIP loyalty program.
